The Vision

Why Northspire Exists

Everything I build comes from the same place.

Photography. Healthcare. Software. Collectibles. Investing. On the surface they look like different worlds. To me they have always been one — different expressions of the same curiosity, each teaching a lesson the others couldn't.

Healthcare taught responsibility. Photography taught observation. Collecting taught patience. Software taught problem-solving. Investing taught me to think in decades.

My path started on the pre-med track at the University at Albany. I thought I already knew where my life was headed. I was wrong — and learning that early turned out to be a gift. Before I became a Registered Nurse, I worked as an EMT, a phlebotomist, and an EKG technician. Those years taught me discipline and what it means to earn someone's trust when they are at their most vulnerable.

A second education was happening away from work. I fell in love with ideas — business, psychology, leadership, philosophy, communication, technology, history. One book became ten, ten became fifty. The more I read, the clearer one truth became: knowledge is worth little until it leaves the page.

So I stopped asking what I should do, and started asking what I should build.

Photography became Geo Lenz Studio. A love of collecting became Astral Collectionz. A problem I kept running into as a collector became MyTCG Ledger. None of them were random. Each one answered a problem I had lived through myself — and each was built to solve it well, not quickly.

In time I saw the larger pattern. I wasn't building separate companies at all. I was building one philosophy, and Northspire is its name: a place where curiosity becomes craftsmanship, where learning becomes action, and where every company earns its place by solving a real problem and being built to last.

I have never chased success the way I chase mastery. I don't ask what's easiest. I ask what's best — and why, and how it truly works. Excellent things tend to follow from that question.

Northspire is still young. Most of what I hope to build doesn't exist yet, and that is the part that excites me most. You aren't seeing the finished story. You're watching the foundation being laid.

Leadership

What healthcare taught me about building

Before Northspire, the work was in emergency rooms and ambulances — as a Registered Nurse in the State of New York and a certified Emergency Medical Technician. That work was never separate from how I lead today. It is the foundation of it.

Calm under pressureIn an emergency, panic helps no one. You learn to steady yourself first, then act. Every company decision is easier when composure comes before reaction.
Decisions with incomplete informationYou rarely have the full picture and still have to choose well. Building a business is the same — you move thoughtfully on what you know, and adjust as you learn more.
Precision & detailIn care, a small detail is the difference that matters. That same attention is why craftsmanship — not shortcuts — defines every Northspire product.
AccountabilityWhen you are responsible for someone's wellbeing, there is nowhere to hide from the outcome. I carry that ownership into everything Northspire ships.
Compassion & serviceHealthcare is service at its most human. Business should be too — companies exist to make people's lives genuinely better, not just to extract from them.
Discipline & continuous learningMedicine never lets you stop learning. Neither does building. The discipline to keep improving is the engine behind long-term thinking.
Trust, earned through actionPatients trust what you do, not what you say. Northspire is built the same way — trust is earned quietly, through the work, over time.

A Letter From the Founder

If you've made it this far, thank you.

You're not just looking at another website — you've taken the time to understand an idea that's still in its earliest chapters.

Northspire wasn't created to build one successful company. It was created to build many. A family of businesses connected by the same values: integrity, craftsmanship, curiosity, and a commitment to creating something genuinely useful.

I've never been interested in chasing shortcuts or overnight success. I'd rather spend years building something people can trust than months building something people forget. Every product, every company, and every decision should exist for a reason. If it doesn't make someone's life better, then it doesn't belong here.

This journey is personal to me.

Every business under Northspire represents something I've dedicated myself to learning — from photography and technology to healthcare, collecting, and entrepreneurship. They may serve different audiences, but they all share the same foundation: solve real problems, keep improving, and never stop learning.

Northspire is still at the beginning.

There is far more ahead than behind, and that's what excites me the most. You're seeing the foundation while it's being built, not the finished structure. Every project, every lesson, and every improvement brings us one step closer to the vision.

So thank you for taking the time to understand what we're building.

This is only the beginning, and I'm excited to have you here to watch the journey unfold. I hope that years from now you'll be able to look back at this page and say, "I remember when it all started."

Welcome to Northspire.

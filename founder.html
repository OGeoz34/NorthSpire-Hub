The Vision

Why Northspire Exists

A holding company, not a portfolio.

People assume a photography studio, a collectibles business, and a piece of software have nothing to do with each other. That assumption is the whole reason Northspire exists.

A portfolio is a list. Things sit next to each other and share an owner. A holding company is different: each business is supposed to make the others better. That is the standard I hold everything to here.

Photography improves branding. Software improves operations. Collectibles teach markets. Healthcare teaches responsibility. Investing teaches capital allocation.

Those aren't slogans, they're the actual mechanics. The images I shoot at Geo Lenz Studio are why Astral Collectionz looks like a brand instead of a storefront. Selling graded cards taught me what buyers really price — which is exactly what MyTCG Ledger had to get right to be worth using. Nursing set the bar for what I consider an acceptable mistake. And every dollar I've had to allocate has made me slower and more deliberate about the next one.

Run a business alone and you learn one industry. Run several with the same hands and you start noticing what carries across — pricing, trust, patience, how people actually decide. Northspire is the structure that lets those lessons compound instead of stranding them in one company.

Every venture strengthens the others. That is the point.

None of that happens on its own. It only works if every company that joins is held to the same bar — which is the part I had to actually write down.

The Standard

How I decide what’s worth building

I’ve passed on more ideas than I’ve started. Usually not because the idea was bad, but because it couldn’t clear this. I keep the list short and I wrote it down, because it’s easy to talk yourself into something when it only lives in your head.

It solves a problem I have hadNot a problem I imagined a market might have. Something I ran into personally, often enough to get tired of it. Invented demand is the most expensive mistake available.
I would pay full price for itIf I would only use it because I made it, it isn’t finished. The bar is whether I’d hand over my own money as a stranger.
The description matches the thingA card is the grade I said it was. A product does what the page claims. This sounds obvious until you notice how much of the market runs on the gap between the two.
It works before it growsAttention is not a fix. Anything scaled early just distributes its flaws faster and to more people.
My name is on itIf someone has a bad experience, one person is responsible for fixing it — usually me. The moment that gets spread across a team, nobody owns it.
It runs without meIf it only works while I’m watching, I didn’t build a business, I built myself a job. The goal is to not be needed day to day.
It still makes sense in ten yearsTrends are a reason to move quickly, not a reason to exist. If the whole thesis evaporates when the moment passes, it was never a company.

A Letter From the Founder

If you've made it this far, thank you.

You're not just looking at another website — you've taken the time to understand an idea that's still in its earliest chapters.

Northspire wasn't created to build one successful company. It was created to build many. A family of businesses connected by the same values: integrity, craftsmanship, curiosity, and a commitment to creating something genuinely useful.

I've never been interested in chasing shortcuts or overnight success. I'd rather spend years building something people can trust than months building something people forget. Every product, every company, and every decision should exist for a reason. If it doesn't make someone's life better, then it doesn't belong here.

This journey is personal to me.

Every business under Northspire represents something I've dedicated myself to learning — from photography and technology to healthcare, collecting, and entrepreneurship. They may serve different audiences, but they all share the same foundation: solve real problems, keep improving, and never stop learning.

Northspire is still at the beginning.

There is far more ahead than behind, and that's what excites me the most. You're seeing the foundation while it's being built, not the finished structure. Every project, every lesson, and every improvement brings us one step closer to the vision.

So thank you for taking the time to understand what we're building.

This is only the beginning, and I'm excited to have you here to watch the journey unfold. I hope that years from now you'll be able to look back at this page and say, "I remember when it all started."

Welcome to Northspire.
